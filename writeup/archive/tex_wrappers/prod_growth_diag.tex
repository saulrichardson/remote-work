\section*{Productivity vs Growth: Stayers vs Composition}

We difference each firm between pre-2020H1 and post-2020H1, limit to fully remote firms, and tag startups as age \(\le 10\), leaving \(N=980\) observations. Two outcomes are studied: a stayer measure (median within-worker rank change for incumbents present in both periods) and an all-worker measure (change in the firm’s average worker rank, capturing composition). Both outcomes are regressed on the firm’s change in growth and a startup indicator, with robust standard errors and weights based on the relevant worker counts; no fixed effects are needed because the outcomes are already differences.

Headline coefficients: stayer slope \(\beta=1.23\) (p = 0.63); all-worker slope \(\beta=6.72\) (p = 0.023).

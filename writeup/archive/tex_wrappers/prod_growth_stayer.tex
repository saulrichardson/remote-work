\section*{Productivity vs Growth (Stayers Only)}

We compare each firm to itself before and after 2020H1 (pre: \(yh<2020\text{H}1\); post: \(yh\ge2020\text{H}1\)). The sample keeps only fully remote firms and marks startups as those age \(\le 10\); this leaves \(N=980\) firms. For the stayer outcome, we track workers who appear in both periods, take their change in rank, and summarize the firm by the median of those within-worker changes.

The regression relates that median change to the firm’s change in growth and a startup indicator:
\[ \Delta\text{Prod}^{\text{stayer}}_f = \alpha + \beta\,\Delta\text{Growth}_f + \gamma\,\text{Startup}_f + \varepsilon_f, \]
using robust standard errors and weights equal to the count of balanced workers. Because the outcome is already a difference, no fixed effects are needed.
